    Mail en Route During the Fighting

    After connections via the postal service were disrupted, senders were sometimes able to have a letter posted in another town by people traveling the countryside. In this case, the letter was taken along from Amsterdam and posted in Apeldoorn 13 days after it was written.

    Nijmegen had finally been liberated on the evening of the fourth day of Operation Market Garden, 21 September 1944 and the postal connection to the north had been severed.

    This letter was sent by Twentol Company in Deventer. Twentol traded in imported oil products, such as lubricating oil. On 10 April 1945, the day of Deventer’s liberation, a number of young Dutch resistance fighters attempted to capture the bridge over the Ijssel. Five resistance members were discovered hiding in the Twentol building and were summarily executed by the Germans, together with a German medic, Ernst Gräwe, who refused to participate in the firing squad. He was shot by his own commander.

    The road bridge which was fought over in September 1944, was the 'Rijnbrug’ Rhine Bridge. Construction of the bridge had begun in 1932 and was completed in 1935. During the German invasion in May 1940, Dutch army engineers blew the bridge to slow the German advance. The Germans then rebuilt the bridge during their occupation of the Netherlands. Work on the road bridge was only just completed in the summer of 1944.

    Kees Adema RDP FRPSL lived with his family at Huissensestraat 13 in Arnhem, just some 300 meters from the southern end of the road bridge. His brother, Piet, was born in the family’s cellar on 17 September 1944, the first day of Operation Market Garden. Their house was taken over by the Germans and used as a lazarett (field hospital), treating wounded German soldiers.

    1. Fallschirm-Armee (1st Parachute Army)

    General Kurt Student (12 May 1890 - 1 July 1978) was in overall command of the Fallschirmjäger, and commanded it throughout the war. In 1944, following the Normandy landings, he was put in charge of the First Paratroop Army and took part in countering the Allied forces near Arnhem.
